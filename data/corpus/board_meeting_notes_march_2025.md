# Board Meeting Notes — March 2025

**Date:** March 26, 2025
**Attendees:** Board members (Sarah Patel, James Liu, Rebecca Torres, Vikram Shah), CEO Alex Chen, CFO Maria Rodriguez, CTO David Park, VP Sales Tom Bradley

## CEO Update (Alex Chen)

Strong start to 2025. Q1 revenue tracking to $36.9M (ahead of plan by 4%). Customer count crossed 750. Key narrative: the market is shifting from "search your enterprise data" to "serve your enterprise data as context to AI." We are well-positioned for this shift.

Three strategic priorities for 2025:
1. Win enterprise — ship permissions, SOC 2, and land 10 new Fortune 500 logos
2. Platform play — launch the Context Budget API and connector marketplace to become the infrastructure layer for enterprise AI
3. Efficient growth — reach EBITDA breakeven by Q2 2026 without additional funding

## CFO Update (Maria Rodriguez)

Cash position: $92.1M. Burn rate trending down (now $5.3M/month, target $4.0M by Q4). Revenue guidance maintained at $170-175M for full year. Discussed potential Series C in Q1 2026 — consensus is to hold off unless growth accelerates beyond current trajectory.

Board approved a $2M increase in R&D budget for H2 to accelerate the Context Budget API and permissions work.

## CTO Update (David Park)

Architecture migration to multi-tenant is 80% complete, expected done by end of Q2. This will reduce infrastructure costs by ~25%. The team is evaluating new embedding models that could improve retrieval quality by 15% while reducing latency.

**Action item for David:** Present a technical proposal for the Context Budget API architecture at the April engineering all-hands. Board wants to see how this becomes a moat.

## VP Sales Update (Tom Bradley)

Pipeline: $52M in qualified opportunities. Win rate for Q1 was 28%, up from 23% in Q4 2024. The competitive landscape is heating up — Glean raised a $200M Series D and is aggressively targeting our mid-market segment.

**Action item for Tom:** Develop a competitive response strategy against Glean by end of April. Focus on our differentiation: API-first approach, context optimization, and developer focus.

## Board Discussion: International Expansion

Preliminary discussion about expanding to Europe in 2026. Key considerations: GDPR compliance (mostly addressed), local hiring, and whether to start with UK or Germany. Board requests a detailed market analysis by next meeting (June 2025).

## Next Meeting

June 25, 2025 — Q2 results review and international expansion proposal.
