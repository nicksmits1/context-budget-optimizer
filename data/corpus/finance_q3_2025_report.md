# Q3 2025 Financial Report

## Revenue Summary

Total revenue for Q3 2025 came in at $47.2M, representing a 14% increase over Q2 2025 ($41.4M) and a 31% year-over-year improvement from Q3 2024 ($36.0M). This marks the fifth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth.

Recurring revenue (ARR) reached $38.1M, accounting for 80.7% of total revenue, up from 76.3% in Q2. Services and implementation revenue contributed the remaining $9.1M.

## Segment Breakdown

**Enterprise Segment:** $29.8M (+18% QoQ)
The enterprise segment drove the majority of growth, fueled by three new Fortune 500 deals closed in September. Average contract value increased to $420K from $385K in Q2.

**Mid-Market Segment:** $12.4M (+8% QoQ)
Mid-market growth was steady, with improved retention rates (94% vs 91% in Q2). The self-serve onboarding flow launched in August contributed 23 new accounts.

**SMB Segment:** $5.0M (+3% QoQ)
SMB growth remained modest. The team is evaluating whether to continue investing in this segment given the higher support costs per dollar of revenue.

## Margins and Profitability

Gross margin improved to 72.4% from 69.8% in Q2, driven by infrastructure cost optimization and the migration to our new multi-tenant architecture. Operating expenses totaled $38.6M, yielding an operating margin of -18.2%, an improvement from -23.1% in Q2.

EBITDA was -$5.8M, trending toward our target of EBITDA breakeven by Q2 2026.

## Cash Position

Cash and equivalents stood at $84.3M at quarter end, with a burn rate of $4.2M/month (down from $5.1M in Q2). At current burn, we have approximately 20 months of runway. The board has approved exploring a Series C raise in Q1 2026 to extend runway and fund international expansion.

## Outlook

Management reaffirms full-year 2025 revenue guidance of $170-175M. Q4 pipeline looks strong with $18M in qualified opportunities, though we expect some deals to slip into Q1 2026 due to enterprise procurement cycles.
