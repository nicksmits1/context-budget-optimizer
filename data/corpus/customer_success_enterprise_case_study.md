# Case Study: MegaCorp Financial

## Company Overview

MegaCorp Financial is a Fortune 500 financial services company with 12,000 employees across 30 offices. They manage $450B in assets and serve institutional and retail clients globally.

## Challenge

MegaCorp's analysts spent an average of 2.8 hours per day searching for information across internal systems. Knowledge was siloed in Confluence, SharePoint, Bloomberg terminals, internal research portals, and email. When analysts used AI assistants, the responses were generic and lacked company-specific context, leading to low trust and adoption.

Key pain points:
- Compliance team couldn't verify that AI-generated advice was grounded in approved internal research
- Research analysts were duplicating work because they couldn't find existing analyses
- New hire ramp-up took 6+ months because institutional knowledge was scattered

## Solution

MegaCorp deployed our platform with connections to 8 data sources: Confluence, SharePoint, internal research database, compliance rulebook, client portfolio system, email (approved threads), Slack (selected channels), and their proprietary market data feed.

Implementation timeline: 6 weeks from contract signing to full production deployment.

## Results (After 6 Months)

- **Time saved:** Analysts reduced information search time from 2.8 hours/day to 0.9 hours/day (68% reduction)
- **AI adoption:** Internal AI assistant usage increased from 15% to 72% of analysts
- **Compliance:** 100% of AI-generated investment recommendations now include source citations from approved materials
- **New hire onboarding:** Ramp-up time reduced from 6 months to 3.5 months
- **ROI:** Estimated $4.2M annual value from productivity gains (based on analyst hourly rates)

## Quote

"The context management layer changed how our team thinks about AI. It's not about having a smarter model — it's about giving the model the right information to work with." — Sarah Mitchell, VP of Digital Innovation, MegaCorp Financial

## Technical Details

- 2.3 million documents indexed
- Average query latency: 340ms (retrieval + context optimization)
- 99.97% uptime over 6 months
- Custom permissions model mapping to their existing Bloomberg terminal access groups
