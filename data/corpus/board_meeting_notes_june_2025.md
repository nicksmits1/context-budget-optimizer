# Board Meeting Notes — June 2025

**Date:** June 25, 2025
**Attendees:** Board members (Sarah Patel, James Liu, Rebecca Torres, Vikram Shah), CEO Alex Chen, CFO Maria Rodriguez, CTO David Park, VP Sales Tom Bradley, VP Product Nina Kapoor

## CEO Update (Alex Chen)

Q2 revenue came in at $41.4M, slightly ahead of plan. Customer count is now 791. The competitive landscape is getting more crowded — Glean's Series D is putting pressure on us in the mid-market. Our response: lean into the developer/API-first angle where they can't compete.

Announced that the Context Budget API shipped in beta to 8 design partners. Early feedback is very positive. This is becoming our key differentiator.

## CTO Update (David Park)

Multi-tenant migration completed on schedule. Infrastructure costs already down 18% (ahead of the 25% target for year-end). The team is now focused on the Context Budget API general availability and the permissions system overhaul.

Presented the Context Budget API architecture as requested from the March board meeting. The board was impressed with the optimization strategies and asked about patentability. David to follow up with legal.

**Action item for David:** Work with legal to evaluate patent potential for the context budget optimization algorithms.

## VP Product Update (Nina Kapoor)

Shared user research findings from the Context Budget API beta. Key insight: enterprise customers want metadata-aware context selection (not just similarity). This validates our importance-weighted strategy approach.

Roadmap update: P0 items on track. SOC 2 audit proceeding smoothly. Advanced permissions in final testing, targeting GA in August.

## International Expansion Discussion

Maria presented the market analysis for European expansion. Recommendation: start with UK in Q2 2026, followed by Germany in Q4 2026. Estimated investment: $3-5M for first year (hiring, legal setup, marketing). Board approved developing a detailed execution plan for review at the September meeting.

## Series C Discussion

Given strong growth trajectory, the board is now leaning toward a proactive Series C in Q1 2026 at a target valuation of $1.5-2B. The goal is to fund international expansion and potentially acquire a smaller competitor in the connectors space. Alex and Maria to begin informal conversations with target investors.

## Next Meeting

September 24, 2025 — Q3 results and international expansion execution plan.
